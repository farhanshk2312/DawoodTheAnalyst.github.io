Analyzing Amazon Reviews Across Product Categories

This project will focus on extracting and analyzing reviews across multiple product categories (e.g., Beauty, Electronics, Home, Books) to uncover how customer preferences, product perceptions, and marketplace dynamics vary across domains.
  • Shaping Up
Customer reviews are one of the richest forms of feedback in e-commerce, containing both quantitative signals (ratings, helpful votes) and qualitative insights (text, product images, user behavior). With the newly released Amazon Reviews 2023 dataset, which spans 571.54 million reviews (a 245.2% increase from the last version), we have an unprecedented opportunity to study customer-product interactions at scale.

The dataset covers reviews from May 1996 to September 2023, making it a living archive of consumer behavior across nearly three decades of e-commerce evolution.

Objectives:



Cross-Category Insights: Compare rating distributions, verified purchase rates, and helpfulness across product categories.

Temporal Trends: Study how customer expectations and sentiment have shifted over time, especially during e-commerce milestones (Prime launch, COVID-19 shopping boom, etc.)

Price vs Satisfaction: Identify how pricing strategies correlate with ratings and review sentiment across categories.

Review Credibility: Detect unusual reviewer behaviors, such as spam/fake reviews, by analyzing helpful votes, review frequency, and text patterns.

Feature Importance: Use product metadata (features, descriptions, categories) to explore what drives higher satisfaction in different domains.

Data Schema (Simplified)


1. Review-Level Data:

rating, title, text, images

asin, parent_asin

user_id, timestamp, helpful_vote, verified_purchase

2. Product-Level Data:

main_category, average_rating, rating_number

features, description, price

store, categories, details

This dual structure allows for joining customer sentiment with product attributes, enabling richer category-level analysis.

Methodology


1. Data Extraction & Loading:

Extract .jsonl.gz files for selected product categories.

Load into Pandas/BigQuery for scalable processing.

2. Preprocessing:

Normalize timestamps into monthly/quarterly aggregates.

Clean text fields (text, features, description) for NLP analysis.

Handle missing values (price, bought_together) with appropriate imputation strategies.

3. Analysis & Modeling:

Exploratory Analysis: rating trends, review lengths, helpful votes.

Sentiment Analysis: NLP-based sentiment vs given star ratings.

Cross-Category Comparison: benchmark categories on average rating, verified purchase ratio, and review helpfulness.

Predictive Modeling: estimate how product attributes (features, price) influence review ratings.

4. Visualization & Storytelling:

Build interactive dashboards (Data Studio / Power BI) showing trends over time.

Highlight category-level differences and anomalies.

Expected Outcomes:


A comprehensive multi-category analysis of Amazon reviews from 1996–2023.

Insights into how customer satisfaction differs across categories and time.

Identification of key product features linked with higher customer ratings.

A case study in handling large-scale semi-structured data (571M+ records).

Ready-to-deploy recommendations dashboard for category managers and marketplace sellers.

This project demonstrates the ability to:


Work with massive datasets efficiently.

Apply advanced analytics and NLP to real-world business problems.

Deliver insights that have practical applications in e-commerce strategy (pricing, product development, review management).

By combining scale (571M reviews) with depth (product metadata + review text), the project will bridge the gap between raw customer opinion and actionable business insight.
