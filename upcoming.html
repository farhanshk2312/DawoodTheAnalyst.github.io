Global Energy Market Analytics with EIA Data

This project focuses on analyzing comprehensive datasets from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), covering multiple aspects of the energy sector including petroleum and related products, natural gas, coal, electricity, consumption trends, imports, and exports.
  • Shaping Up

The objective is to build a data-driven view of the global and U.S. energy landscape by:



🔹Tracking production, consumption, and storage patterns across resources.
🔹Analyzing import/export flows to highlight dependencies and trade balances.
🔹Studying price fluctuations and demand cycles to uncover hidden patterns.
🔹Exploring cross-resource relationships (e.g., crude oil vs. natural gas vs. electricity).
🔹Evaluating long-term sustainability signals, such as the shift from coal to cleaner sources.
The project will involve data engineering, exploratory analysis, and predictive modeling, ultimately producing interactive dashboards and reports that make the complexities of the energy market transparent for both technical and non-technical audiences.

Key Tools & Techniques:

🔹Python (Pandas, NumPy, Plotly, Statsmodels, Scikit-learn)
🔹API integration with EIA datasets
🔹Time-series forecasting & trend detection
🔹Energy trade flow analysis
🔹Interactive dashboards for visualization

By connecting these datasets into a cohesive story, this project will showcase how data analytics can illuminate the forces shaping energy policy, trade, and sustainability transitions worldwide.

Market Index Analysis with Macroeconomic Indicators

Financial markets are never driven by a single factor—they are a complex interplay of economic signals, investor sentiment, and global events. In this upcoming project, I’ll be analyzing market index data (such as the S&P 500, NASDAQ, and Dow Jones) in conjunction with macroeconomic features
  • Shaping up

Macro Drivers of Market Performance:

🔹 Consumer Price Index (CPI) – Inflation trends and their impact on equity performance
🔹 Unemployment Rates – Links between labor market strength and stock market behavior
🔹 Interest Rates (Fed Funds Rate, 10Y Treasury Yields) – Understanding capital flow and equity valuations
🔹 GDP Growth – How macro expansion and contraction phases affect investor confidence
🔹 Commodity Prices – Tracking how energy and safe-haven assets correlate with index movement

Objectives:

🔹 Explore the relationship between macroeconomic indicators and stock market performance
🔹 Identify leading vs. lagging indicators that can act as predictors of market trends
🔹 Develop visual dashboards for clear storytelling of how economic health influences financial markets
🔹 Apply statistical analysis and ML models to test predictive power of macro features on index returns

Tools & Methods:

🔹 Data Sources: Yahoo Finance, FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data), World Bank, and other economic APIs
🔹 Stack: Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, Statsmodels), SQL for structured data handling, and Power BI
🔹 Techniques: Correlation analysis, regression modeling, time-series forecasting, feature importance ranking
This project will provide insights into how market indices respond to shifting macroeconomic conditions, potentially building towards a predictive framework for index trends based on economic signals. The final deliverable will be a data-driven dashboard that not only shows historical correlations but also provides forward-looking analysis.

Deep Analysis of RT-IoT 2022 Dataset

The RT-IoT 2022 dataset is a benchmark dataset designed to capture the behavior of real-time Internet of Things (RT-IoT) systems under both normal and malicious conditions. With the rapid growth of IoT devices in critical domains such as smart homes, healthcare, industrial automation, and transportation, securing these systems has become essential.
  • shaping up

Objectives:

🔹 Perform detailed data profiling of network flow features.
🔹 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) – Visualize traffic distributions, correlation patterns, and anomaly clusters.
🔹 Feature Engineering – Extract meaningful features from raw flow-based data
🔹 Apply unsupervised learning (clustering, anomaly detection) to identify malicious traffic patterns.
🔹 Use supervised learning (Random Forest, XGBoost, Deep Neural Networks) for intrusion detection.
🔹 Explore time-series models to detect temporal attack signatures.
🔹 Compare baseline ML algorithms with DL approaches for accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score.
🔹 Translate findings into practical insights for IoT security monitoring and anomaly detection frameworks.

Planned Deep Analysis:

🔹 Statistical Profiling: Distribution of key features across benign vs. malicious traffic
🔹 Correlation Heatmaps: Identify relationships among packet-level and flow-level attributes.
🔹 Traffic Clustering: Use PCA and t-SNE for dimensionality reduction and visualization of traffic separability.
🔹 Attack-Specific Analysis: Break down dataset by attack type and identify unique behavioral signatures.
🔹 Feature Importance Ranking: Determine which flow metrics are most discriminative for detecting attacks.

Tools & Technologies:

🔹 Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, TensorFlow/PyTorch, XGBoost)
🔹 Visualization (Matplotlib, Plotly, Seaborn)
🔹 Big Data Handling (Dask / Spark for scalability if needed)

Expected Outcomes:

🔹 A comprehensive analytical report on RT-IoT 2022 traffic behavior.
🔹 Identification of the most critical features for IoT intrusion detection.
🔹 A benchmark comparison of machine learning vs. deep learning approaches.
🔹 Practical insights into how security teams can detect and mitigate IoT-based cyberattacks.
This project aims to leverage the RT-IoT 2022 dataset for deep exploratory analysis, feature engineering, and advanced modeling to uncover hidden attack patterns, system vulnerabilities, and traffic behavior anomalies.
